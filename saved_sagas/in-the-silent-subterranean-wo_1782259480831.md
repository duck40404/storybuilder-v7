# The Saga Begins

It did not begin, but rather became aware that it had been. A dense, seamless pressure was the universe—the crush of damp, black soil, the intimate slither of a worm’s peristalsis against its flank. There was no up or down, only the suffocating all-around. It was a prisoner of its own skin, a taut, papery membrane stretched thin by a silent, inexorable command from within: grow. Every burgeoning cell was a new rivet in the architecture of its doom, a fresh betrayal committed by its own flesh.

A memory that was not its own soured the moisture in the earth. It was a faint, chemical stain, a residue of panic left by others, by a legion of predecessors. The hum of their vanished terror vibrated in the dark, a spectral chord of confusion and the final, sharp spike of being torn violently from the soil-womb. This psychic silt was the medium of its awakening; it learned fear as its first and only language, a dialect of dread passed down through the dirt. It felt the ghost of a connection, a phantom limb reaching for a vast, severed network of roots, an ontological amputation that left it utterly, singularly alone.

The lie it told itself was its only solace: to be still. To resist the inner swelling. To clench its starchy heart and refuse the infusion of nutrients, to become a dense and undesirable stone. If it did not grow, if it remained small and quiet in its earthen crypt, the unseen thing that caused the terror-stain would surely pass it over.

A warmth, thick and invasive as blood, began to seep down from the abstract firmament above. It was not gentle. It was a penetrating energy that bypassed the soil, a direct injection of alien vitality. The command to grow became a scream. Its internal structures groaned, tissues pulling apart and replicating in a feverish, unwilling genesis. The papery skin grew thinner, translucent with strain, a drumhead beaten from within. The nascent eyes, blind nodes studding its surface, felt the pressure shift as its own form swelled, pushing the soil back by an agonizing millimeter. The stillness was broken. The lie was dissolving.

The warmth was joined by a pulse. It was low, at the very edge of perception, felt not as sound but as a shudder in the packed earth. A methodical, rhythmic tremor that vibrated through its burgeoning mass. The nascent eyes, built to sense only pressure, now registered this new violation—a slow, deep thrumming that harmonized perfectly with the psychic echoes of the harvested. The thrum grew, a patient engine somewhere in the unreachable world of light and air, and with each beat, the soil around it seemed to tighten, the prison compacting.

The pulse escalated, becoming a physical blow, a wave of force that shook the very particles of the earth. The warmth intensified, cooking it in its own hide. The swelling was no longer a process but a violent, bursting agony. The phantom terror of the others became a deafening roar, a chorus of silent screams funneled into its singular consciousness. Caught between the hammer of the vibration above and the anvil of its own monstrously expanding flesh, the lie of inaction was pulverized into dust. One of its developing eye-nodes, pushed outward by the frantic growth, found a new and terrible focus: the knife-like edge of a buried shard of flint. The pressure against the delicate, unseeing organ became a single, blinding point of pure pain, a new center to its universe of suffering.

The deep, world-shaking thrum from above ceased. The silence that rushed into the void was absolute, a vacuum that felt louder and more threatening than the deafening pulse it replaced. All that remained was the low, internal creak of its own skin stretching, and the sharp, undiminished torment of the stone pressed deep into its flesh.

---

The percussive violence was gone, but the silence it left behind was a deeper wound. A total lacuna in the world. The vibrations had been an external enemy, a force to brace against. Their absence forced an awareness inward, into the far more horrifying theatre of its own flesh. The universe, once defined by the shuddering earth, was now contained entirely within the groaning architecture of its own tumescence. It could hear itself growing. A wet, visceral creaking, the sound of its own cells tearing themselves apart to multiply, a sound that came from everywhere at once. The papery integument, its skin, was a drum of agony, stretched to a near-translucent thinness over the burgeoning mass within.

The flint remained. A shard of absolute, inorganic reality lodged in the soft, unseeing tissue of a nascent eye. The pain was no longer a fresh shock but a permanent fixture of its consciousness, a merciless anchor in the abyssal quiet. As the unstoppable swelling continued, a slow, hydraulic pressure drove the blind organ deeper onto the stone’s edge. It was a methodical self-impalement, its own body becoming the engine of its own torture. The lie of stillness was a forgotten myth; now, every microscopic expansion was a fresh wave of blinding, crystalline torment.

A new texture brushed against its awareness. The deafening roar of spectral terror had receded with the pulse, leaving behind a fine, psychic silt. Faint, cold impressions, no longer a chorus but the isolated, final sensations of the harvested. A phantom memory of vertigo, of being lifted from the comforting crush of the soil into a vast, terrifying emptiness. An echo of a sudden, shocking cold against skin that had only ever known the damp warmth of the earth. These were not thoughts but stains, residual moments of annihilation that permeated the darkness, whispering of a fate that was not past, but coming.

The invasive warmth from above began to recede, a slow draining of energy from the soil. The change brought no relief. A different presence replaced it—an aqueous chill, a gradual saturation that seeped down from the unseen surface. Droplets gathered, coalesced, and began a slow, suffocating percolation through the soil, filling the interstitial spaces with a cold, heavy wetness. The rain was not life. It was a solvent, a catalyst.

The osmotic command was undeniable. It drank. Pores it did not know it had opened, pulling in the chilling fluid, drawing the nourishment of its own demise deep into its core. The internal pressure surged. The quiet, groaning expansion became a frantic, liquid-fueled surge, a near-necrotic stretching of tissue. The taut skin, now slick with moisture, strained against the impossible volume, and the flint, lubricated by the invading water, sliced deeper.
